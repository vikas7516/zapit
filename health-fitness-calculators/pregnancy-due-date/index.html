Current Trimester Information

First Trimester (Weeks 1-12)

The first trimester is crucial for baby's organ development. You may experience morning sickness, fatigue, and breast tenderness.

Key Developments:
  • Neural tube develops into brain and spinal cord
  • Heart begins to beat around week 6
  • Major organs start forming
  • Baby's size: From poppy seed to lime

Second Trimester (Weeks 13-26)

Often called the "golden period" - energy returns, morning sickness typically subsides, and you may start feeling baby's movements.

Key Developments:
  • Gender can be determined via ultrasound
  • Baby develops hearing and can respond to sounds
  • Fingerprints form
  • Baby's size: From lemon to corn cob

Third Trimester (Weeks 27-40)

Baby's major development is complete, now focusing on growth and weight gain. You may experience increased discomfort as baby grows.

Key Developments:
  • Brain development accelerates
  • Lungs mature for breathing
  • Baby positions for birth
  • Baby's size: From cauliflower to watermelon

About Pregnancy Calculators

How Accurate Are Due Dates?

Due dates are estimates. Only about 5% of babies are born on their exact due date. Most babies arrive within 2 weeks before or after the calculated date.

Different Calculation Methods

  • LMP Method: Most common, assumes ovulation 14 days after last period
  • Conception Date: More accurate if you know when conception occurred
  • Ultrasound: Most accurate in first trimester

Important Notes

  • Full-term pregnancy ranges from 37-42 weeks
  • Early ultrasounds are most accurate for dating
  • Irregular cycles may affect LMP accuracy
  • Always consult your healthcare provider
